Our Commitment: TechConnect NZ is dedicated to providing a harassment-free, supportive environment for all participants, regardless of gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, or religion. We value diversity, respect, and inclusion in all interactions within our community.

1. Purpose and Scope

This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all participants in the TechConnect NZ community, including mentors, mentees, staff, and any other individuals involved in our platform or associated events.

It applies to all spaces managed by TechConnect NZ, including but not limited to:

  • Our online platform and all interactions within it
  • Direct messages and communications between participants
  • Mentorship meetings and sessions (virtual or in-person)
  • Community events, workshops, and gatherings
  • Social media channels and public forums managed by TechConnect NZ

By participating in our community, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct and cooperate with the organizers and administrators who enforce it.

2. Core Values

Our community is built upon the following core values:

Inclusivity and Belonging

We strive to create a space where women and non-binary individuals from all backgrounds feel welcome, valued, and empowered. We acknowledge the importance of diversity in tech and work to actively include underrepresented perspectives.

Respect and Dignity

We treat all individuals with respect and dignity, honoring differences in opinion, background, and experience. We value active listening and thoughtful communication.

Manaakitanga

We embrace the Māori concept of manaakitanga, showing kindness, respect, and care toward others. We place importance on hospitality, generosity, and mutual respect in all community interactions.

Whanaungatanga

We foster relationship building and a sense of connectedness among our community members, recognizing that meaningful connections form the foundation of effective mentorship.

Growth and Learning

We create space for ongoing learning, skill development, and personal growth. We recognize that everyone has something to teach and something to learn.

Confidentiality and Trust

We respect privacy and maintain appropriate confidentiality in our discussions and relationships, creating a foundation of trust within our community.

3. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

  • Be respectful and inclusive in your language and actions
  • Practice active listening and seek to understand before responding
  • Be open to learning about different perspectives and experiences
  • Honor commitments made to mentorship partners and the community
  • Communicate clearly and professionally, especially when providing feedback
  • Respect boundaries established by others
  • Maintain confidentiality when information is shared in confidence
  • Acknowledge and correct mistakes when they occur
  • Focus on collaboration rather than competition
  • Welcome newcomers and help them feel included
  • Speak up when you witness behavior that contradicts our community values

For Mentors: Remember the influence you hold as a mentor. Practice active listening, provide constructive feedback, respect your mentee's autonomy, and be mindful of potential power dynamics in the relationship.

For Mentees: Approach mentorship with openness to learning, respect your mentor's time and expertise, communicate your needs clearly, and provide feedback about what is and isn't working in the mentorship relationship.

4. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable within our community:

Harassment and Discrimination

  • Discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior
  • Offensive comments related to gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, race, ethnicity, or religion
  • Unwelcome sexual attention, comments, or imagery
  • Deliberate misgendering or using rejected names or pronouns
  • Persistent unwanted contact or attention after requests to cease

Disruptive or Harmful Behavior

  • Intimidation, bullying, or threatening behavior
  • Violent or aggressive language or actions
  • Sharing others' personal or private information without consent (doxxing)
  • Posting or displaying sexually explicit or violent material
  • Repeatedly disruptive behavior in community spaces
  • Any behavior that would reasonably make someone feel unsafe, uncomfortable, or unwelcome

Professional Integrity Violations

  • Soliciting or offering services that violate our Terms of Service
  • Using the platform primarily for sales, recruitment, or other commercial purposes without permission
  • Misrepresenting qualifications, experience, or identity
  • Breaching confidentiality agreements
  • Engaging in conflicts of interest without disclosure

Zero Tolerance: TechConnect NZ has zero tolerance for any form of harassment, discrimination, or abusive behavior. Violations of this policy may result in immediate removal from the platform.

5. Cultural Considerations

TechConnect NZ is committed to honoring the bicultural foundation of Aotearoa New Zealand and creating a platform that respects and incorporates Te Ao Māori (the Māori worldview) alongside other cultural perspectives.

Te Tiriti o Waitangi Principles

We acknowledge the importance of partnership, participation, and protection as outlined in Te Tiriti o Waitangi (The Treaty of Waitangi) and strive to incorporate these principles into our community governance.

Cultural Respect

We expect all community members to:

  • Respect cultural protocols and practices
  • Acknowledge and appreciate cultural differences
  • Avoid cultural appropriation
  • Be open to learning about different cultural perspectives
  • Recognize that cultural norms around communication and mentorship may vary

Te Reo Māori

We encourage the use of Te Reo Māori alongside English in our community spaces, while ensuring inclusion for those who may not speak the language.

6. Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to report it. Your safety and well-being are our priority.

How to Report

You can report Code of Conduct violations through the following channels:

  • Platform reporting feature: Use the "Report a Concern" button available on all profile pages and in messaging interfaces
  • Email: Send a confidential email to conduct@techconnect.nz
  • Contact form: Fill out the secure form on our Contact page

What to Include in a Report

When making a report, please include:

  • Your contact information (if you're comfortable providing it)
  • Names of the individuals involved
  • Description of the incident or behavior
  • Date, time, and location/context of the incident
  • Any relevant screenshots, messages, or other documentation
  • Names of any witnesses (if applicable)
  • Any additional information that may be helpful

What Happens After You Report

After receiving a report, our response team will:

  1. Acknowledge receipt of your report within 24 hours
  2. Review the information provided
  3. Conduct a fair and thorough investigation
  4. Maintain confidentiality to the extent possible
  5. Take appropriate action based on the findings
  6. Provide you with an update on the resolution (where appropriate)

Confidentiality: We will respect your privacy and maintain confidentiality throughout the reporting process. Anonymous reports will be accepted, though they may limit our ability to investigate and respond.

7. Consequences of Violations

TechConnect NZ takes all reports of Code of Conduct violations seriously. Consequences for violations will be determined based on the severity, impact, and history of the behavior.

Potential Actions

Actions may include, but are not limited to:

  • Verbal or written warning with clarification of expected behavior
  • Temporary restriction from specific features or areas of the platform
  • Temporary suspension from the platform for a defined period
  • Permanent removal from the platform
  • Exclusion from future TechConnect NZ events
  • Other actions as deemed appropriate to the situation

Enforcement Principles

When enforcing this Code of Conduct, we will:

  • Conduct fair and impartial investigations
  • Consider the impact of the behavior on affected individuals and the community
  • Focus on education and restoration where appropriate
  • Take more severe action for repeated violations or particularly harmful behavior
  • Protect the identities of those who report violations when possible

TechConnect NZ reserves the right to remove any individual from the platform for any reason if their presence or behavior is deemed detrimental to the community.

8. Safety and Privacy

Ensuring the safety and privacy of our community members is paramount.

Personal Safety

We recommend the following safety practices:

  • For initial meetings, use our platform's video conferencing tools or meet in public places
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information, especially in early interactions
  • Trust your instincts – if something feels wrong, it probably is
  • Set clear boundaries regarding communication methods and frequency
  • Report any concerning behavior promptly

Privacy Considerations

To protect privacy:

  • Do not share others' personal information without explicit permission
  • Respect confidentiality in mentorship conversations
  • Do not record meetings without consent from all participants
  • Be mindful of what personal information you share on your profile
  • Review our Privacy Policy for details on how we handle your data

Safety First: If you ever feel unsafe or uncomfortable in a mentorship relationship, prioritize your wellbeing. You can end any conversation or relationship at any time without explanation.

9. Implementation and Review

This Code of Conduct is not just a document; it's a commitment to creating and maintaining a positive community culture.

Implementation

We implement this Code of Conduct through:

  • Clear communication of expectations during onboarding
  • Regular reminders and educational resources
  • Accessible reporting mechanisms
  • Trained moderators and administrators
  • Consistent enforcement of standards

Review Process

This Code of Conduct will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains effective and relevant. We welcome community feedback on ways to improve our policies and practices.
