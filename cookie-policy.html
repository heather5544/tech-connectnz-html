2. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent cookies remain on your device after you close your browser and can be used by the website on subsequent visits. These cookies expire after a specified period or can be manually deleted.
  • Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. These cookies help websites recognize you as the same user throughout your visit.

Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies, including:

  • Web beacons: Small graphic images (also known as "pixel tags" or "clear GIFs") that may be included on our Services and used to track user navigation and web browsing behavior.
  • Local storage: Similar to cookies but with greater capacity and typically stored on your device, not your browser.
  • Device fingerprinting: Collection and analysis of information from your device, such as your browser type, operating system, and installed plugins, to create a unique identifier.

For simplicity, we refer to all these technologies as "cookies" in this policy.

3. Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies for various purposes. The cookies we use fall into the following categories:

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for you to browse our website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the site. Without these cookies, certain services cannot be provided.

Functional Cookies

These cookies enable our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. They may also be used to provide services you have requested, such as watching a video or commenting on a blog.

Performance/Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how you use our website, such as which pages you visit most often and if you receive error messages. They help us improve how our website works and make it easier for you to navigate.

Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver content that is more relevant to you and your interests. They may be placed on our website by our advertising partners and used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other websites.

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling certain cookies may impact your experience on our website. Here's what might happen if you disable different types of cookies:

If You Disable Strictly Necessary Cookies

You may not be able to:

  • Log in to your account
  • Access secure areas of the platform
  • Use the mentorship matching features
  • Submit forms or save preferences

If You Disable Functional Cookies

You may experience:

  • Loss of customized settings (e.g., language preferences)
  • Inability to save accessibility settings between sessions
  • Reduced personalization features

If You Disable Analytics Cookies

There will be minimal impact on your browsing experience, but:

  • We won't be able to analyze how you use our site
  • We may not be able to identify and fix usability issues
  • We'll have less information to improve our platform based on user behavior

If You Disable Targeting Cookies

You may see:

  • Less relevant content and recommendations
  • Generic rather than personalized messaging
